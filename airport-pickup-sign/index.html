Where to meet passengers, terminal by terminal, at the world's busiest airports for chauffeur work. Each guide has verified meet-and-greet zone information.

Mr Smith

Sydney Kingsford Smith (SYD)

T1 Pre-booked Transport Meeting Point at Allpress Cafe, Priority Pick-up e-Tag zone, T2/T3 carousel meets.

Melbourne Tullamarine (MEL)

Chauffeur meeting points inside all four terminals. T123 Transport Hub changes coming Spring 2026.

Auckland Airport (AKL)

Separate International and Domestic terminals, biosecurity adds 60 minutes, new pickup zone open.

Dublin Airport (DUB)

T1 short-haul, T2 long-haul, VIP parking 2 minutes from the door, US Preclearance for departures.

London Heathrow (LHR)

No forecourt waiting. Short Stay collection rows at every terminal. ULEZ-compliant vehicles only.

Singapore Changi (SIN)

Signposted Meet & Greet zones in T1, T2, T3, T4. Kerbside waiting is fined. Multi-storey for longer holds.

New York JFK (JFK)

Chauffeurs meet inside terminal baggage claim with a name sign. Different meet point per terminal.

Los Angeles LAX (LAX)

Lower-level kerbside for premium tiers, LAX-it shuttle for other rideshare. Meet & Greet inside baggage claim.

Rome Fiumicino (FCO)

Kerbside pickup prohibited. Meet in the Main Waiting Hall at the LIMO DRIVER and N.C.C. columns.

Milan Malpensa (MXP)

Two terminals 5 km apart. 15-minute zone with plate recognition. Free 60-minute waiting between T1 and T2.

Madrid Barajas (MAD)

Per-terminal meet points: T1 Post Office, T2 Aena green umbrella, T4 Arrival Lounge near Bar Bareto.

Barcelona El Prat (BCN)

No baggage hall access for drivers. Wait in the arrivals hall after customs with a name sign.
